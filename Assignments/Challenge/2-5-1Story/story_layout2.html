Wait...You Eat Garbage!?

Yes, and proudly. The Garbage Plate is the signature dish of Rochester, NY, and despite its offputting name, it is delicious! If you've ever been to a summer picnic and piled your plate high with all the great food on offer, you may have eaten one without even knowing it. At its most basic, a Garbage Plate is a big serving each of homefries and macaroni salad with a hamburger or hot dog laid on top.

Build A Foundation With Fries

To get things started, half of the dish is set up with a heaping helping of home fries. These little potato nuggets, fried up crispy, act as the unifying agent of a proper Plate. The salty but otherwise unintrusive fries will complement each bite without overtaking any of the other flavors.

Put Some Mac In Your Snack

Sharing the foundational levels, a mayo based macaroni salad fills up the other half of the bottom of the dish. This creamy side will add a little richness and a great contrast of hot vs cold once we dig in. Importantly, this is a more East Coast macaroni salad preperation, with a much less vinegary flavor profile and very few added veggies.

The Main Event: Pick Your Meat

The biggest personalization point of a Plate is the main protein that will sit atop your carbs like a regent upon a throne. The most common choices are two cheeseburger patties or two hot dogs. No buns, just the meat. To mix things up, many will get a combo, and do one of each.

Dress (Your Plate) For Success

Lastly, the condements. Since your Plate is, essentially, just a huge deconstructed picnic, you can treat it almost the same as if you were dressing up a burger. Ketchup, mustard, relish, and diced onion are all traditional fixins for Garbage Plates. And then, the secret ingredient. Rochester's "Meat Hot", a slightly spicy ground beef hotsauce. Get a big laddel spooned over the top, and let it add just the right amount of kick to every bite.
